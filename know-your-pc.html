Know Your PC

Power Supply (PSU)

The power supply is like the heart of your computer. It provides electrical power to all the components, allowing them to work.


Motherboard

Think of the motherboard as the central nervous system. It's a big circuit board where all the other parts connect. It allows different parts of your computer to communicate with each other.


Central Processing Unit (CPU)

The CPU is the brain of your computer. It performs calculations and processes instructions from software, allowing you to run programs and perform tasks.


Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

The GPU is like the artist of your computer. It's responsible for rendering images and graphics, making everything you see on your screen look nice and smooth.


Random Access Memory (RAM)

RAM is like your computer's short-term memory. It stores data and instructions that the CPU needs to access quickly while running programs. The more RAM you have, the more tasks your computer can handle at once.


Storage

Storage comes in two main types: Hard Disk Drives (HDD) and Solid State Drives (SSD). HDDs are slower and cheaper, using spinning disks for storage. SSDs, on the other hand, are faster and more durable, utilizing flash memory.
